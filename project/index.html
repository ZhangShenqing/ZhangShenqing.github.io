Ames图书馆数字人文学者项目 - 2024年暑期
| Digital Humanities Fellowship at Ames Library - Summer 2024

「Seeds of Change」是McLean郡历史博物馆和伊利诺伊卫斯理大学的Ames图书馆合作开展的暑期数字人文学者项目。该项目旨在利用访客到访McLean郡历史博物馆的机会，向公众推广McLean郡的本土生植物，并普及本土生植物对生态环境的多方面影响。该项目通过跨平台功能的实现和网页趣味活动，向访客介绍博物馆广场上所种植的本土生植物，主要面向16岁以下会使用手机的孩童。

在该项目中，我的工作内容包括：

  • 收集有关本土生植物益处的数据并制作宣传内容。
  • 与项目合作双方负责人保持有效、及时的沟通，确保项目顺利向前推动。
  • 利用既有建站框架「Hexo」和主机服务提供商「Reclaim Hosting」实现项目网站落地。
  • 根据孩童的审美，设计网页的交互，确保内容有趣，易于理解并照顾不同年龄层受众群体。

“Seeds of Change” is a summer digital humanities fellowship project launched by the McLean County Museum of History and Ames Library at Illinois Wesleyan University. The project aims to use the opportunity of visitors to the McLean County Museum of History to promote the local indigenous plants of McLean County to the public and to educate on the various impacts of indigenous plants on the ecological environment. Through cross-platform functionality and engaging web activities, the project introduces visitors to the indigenous plants planted on the museum plaza, primarily targeting children under the age of 16 who use mobile phones.

In this project, my work includes:

  • Collecting data on the benefits of indigenous plants and designing promotional content.
  • Maintaining timely communication with project collaborators to ensure a smooth progress of the project.
  • Implementing the project website using the existing website framework “Hexo” and the hosting service provider “Reclaim Hosting”.
  • Designing web pages tailored to children’s aesthetics, ensuring the content is engaging, easy to understand and covering audiences of different age groups.
