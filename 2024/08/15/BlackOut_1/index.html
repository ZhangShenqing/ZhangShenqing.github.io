一双靴子，死死得被像是带着吸盘的触手一样的泥浆裹在泡着蛆虫和残肢的的战壕里，它的主人双手撑着被炮弹犁了几十遍的土堆，费劲了力气也没能让它从大地母亲的怀抱里挣脱。

A pair of boots was tightly engulfed by mud that clung like suction-cupped tentacles in a trench soaked with maggots and severed limbs. Their owner, hands braced against a dirt mound plowed by countless shells, struggled in vain to free them from the Mother Earth’s grasp.

翻浆期的乌东平原在让人绝望这一点上从不让人失望。

The Plains in East Ukraine never fail to deliver despair in their churning season.

不远处的炮火余震仍在地平线上回荡，仿佛某种充满恶意的嘲笑——“死神想和你谈谈了。”瓦西里蜷缩在战壕里，一阵无力的绝望涌上心头。十年前，他在基辅第聂伯河大坝上慰问从前线归来的伤兵时，怎么也不会想到这场战争竟然持续了整整十年，直到当年的「小瓦西里」变成了今天的士官瓦西里，直到两个国家解体，分裂成无数个公社，组成了两个敌对的自治同盟。

The distant rumble of artillery still echoed on the horizon, like a malicious sneer—“Death wants a word with you.” Huddled in the trench, Vasily felt a wave of helpless despair. Ten years ago, when he was visiting wounded soldiers recovering from the frontlines at the Dnieper Dam in Kyiv, he never imagined this war would drag on for a full decade—long enough for young Vasily to become Sergeant Vasily, and for two nations to fracture into countless communes forming two opposing federations.

“也许仇恨这种东西和政治无关，只和果戈里这位老掉牙的作家的归属权有关。” 瓦西里苦涩的幽默感在此刻显得格外不合时宜。他已经被沾满血污的泥浆困住了一个小时，再这样下去，双腿恐怕会坏死——那将是后方医院的医生乐于见到的「省事结局」。

“Maybe hatred isn’t about politics at all, but about who claims this old relic of a writer like Gogol.” Vasily’s grim sense of humor couldn’t have chosen a worse moment to surface. He had been stuck in the blood-soaked mud for an hour; if this dragged on any longer, his legs might start to rot—a ‘convenient outcome’ that the doctors back at the field hospital would no doubt appreciate.

更糟糕的是，瓦西里鬼使神差地仰望天空，正准备问候上帝时，视线里捕捉到了一个正朝他飞来的「天使」。那个来自天堂的使者在广阔的天幕中显得渺小，不过是个黑点，却带着神祇的旨意在天际盘旋。“神的使者终于注意到我了？带我走吧。”瓦西里的祷告前所未有的虔诚。空气中传来阵阵颤动，「天使」似乎锁定了他。“就这样，再靠近一点。”

To make matters worse, Vasily instinctively looked up at the sky, intending to curse at God, when his eyes caught sight of an “angel” flying toward him. That “messenger from heaven” was barely a speck against the vast sky, carrying divine orders as it circled the firmament. “Has God’s envoy finally noticed me? Take me away, please.” Vasily’s prayer had never been so sincere. The air began to vibrate; the “angel” seemed to fix its gaze on him. “Just a little closer.”

那「天使」在空中机械地画了个圈，随即俯冲，直奔战壕中这个孤单的士官。瓦西里只觉得头顶的空气仿佛一层层海绵，被「天使」无情地切开。随着那几乎刺穿耳膜的尖啸声愈发逼近，他终于感受到这位使者不可抗拒的杀气。

The “angel” traced a cold, mechanical circle in the air before diving straight at the lone sergeant in the trench. Vasily felt the air above him splitting apart like layers of sponge, sliced by the “angel” without mercy. As the piercing whistle grew closer, he finally sensed the inevitable, lethal intent of this messenger.

他紧盯着那个黑点，倒吸一口凉气，话已说不出口，脑海里只有震耳欲聋的呐喊：“那是无人机！”

He stared at the black dot, gasping, unable to speak, with only one deafening thought in his mind: “That’s a drone!”

惊魂未定的男人从慌神中惊醒，大口地吞吐着不再有腐尸气味的空气，带着被冷汗盖住的身体环顾四周——他还在该死的慕尼黑安全屋。

Startled, Vasily jolted awake, gasping for air, grateful the stench of rotting corpses was gone. He was drenched in cold sweat, scanning his surroundings—still trapped in that damn safehouse in Munich.

“知道我们的真实身份让你被吓走了神，瓦西里先生。你的「灵知」一如我们所料。”房间里的电脑传出低语。

“The revelation of our true identities shook your faith, didn’t it, Mr. Vasily? Your ‘gnosis’ turned out just as we expected,” whispered the voice from the room’s computer.

瓦西里强撑着被冷汗浸入的眼睛，盯着显示屏，报以惊恐的沉默……

Vasily fought against the cold sweat, staring at the screen, his silence thick with fear.
