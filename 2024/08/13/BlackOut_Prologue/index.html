「Black Out」• 序｜"Black Out" • Prologue

本文灵感来源于周未凡的「人工智能纪元」｜部分英文翻译由「ChatGPT-4」生成
This is a continuation inspired by Weifan Zhou’s “Black Out”.
Part of its translation is generated by ChatGPT-4.

“ити нахуй” 三十出头的逃兵咒骂一声，从地板上坐了起来，茫然地看着他眼前的电脑荧幕。他的灵魂就和他现在的身体一样，像是典型的酒后斯拉夫人。

“Fuck off,” the deserter in his thirties cursed as he sat up from the floor, staring blankly at the computer screen in front of him. His soul, like his current state, resembled a typical drunken Slav.

“主显节假期可不是一个适合开这种玩笑的日子。” 他朝着空荡的房间喊道。二十四小时前？又或许他妈的几个小时以前，他在扎波罗热前线那个每天与老鼠睡在一起的兵营里下定决心离开，日子就定在主显节，因为前线照例要停火，哨位上的大头兵们也会允许自己走神。

“The Epiphany holiday is not a day for jokes like this,” he shouted into the empty room. Twenty-four hours ago? Maybe a few damn hours ago, he had made up his mind to leave the Zaporozhye frontline, where he shared his quarters with rats daily. He chose the Epiphany because, as usual, the front would cease fire, and the soldiers on duty would allow themselves to daydream.

交战双方彼此心照不宣的开小差便宜了他这个有着不少前科的溜号者。“我本该在前往摩尔多瓦的快艇上……”

The tacit agreement between both sides to slack off worked in favor of a deserter with quite a record. “I should be on a speedboat to Moldova…”

“瓦西里·罗曼诺夫维奇·别连科先生……” 电脑里传来一阵难听到令人发指的低语，像是从潮湿的洞窟里传来，裹挟着水汽被吹进他的耳膜，打断了他似乎没法停下来的抱怨。“我希望你能原谅我们的冒昧与无礼。” 电子元件背后的声音顿了顿，像是打了个嗝。“如您在屏幕上所见，我们用这样的方式请您来这里，是希望您能帮助我们，黑掉那个「地球委员会」背后的「提线者」———你们称之为AI的东西。”

“Mr. Vasily Romanovich Belenko…” A horribly unpleasant whisper emanated from the computer, sounding as if it were coming from a damp cavern, carried on a gust of moisture into his eardrums, interrupting his seemingly endless complaints. “I hope you can forgive our intrusion and rudeness.” The voice behind the electronic components paused as if it hiccuped. “As you can see on the screen, we brought you here to ask for your help in hacking the puppet master behind the ‘Earth Committee’—what you call AI.”

“您现在正处于慕尼黑远郊的一座安全屋内。请相信我，把您带到这里是为了您的安全。当然，这也让您省去了在快艇上朝摩尔多瓦奔去时的不悦体验。“

“You are now in a safe house on the outskirts of Munich. Believe me, bringing you here was for your safety. It also spares you the unpleasant experience of rushing towards Moldova on a speedboat.”

“你怎么……”

“How did you…”

“您可以认为我们知道关于您的一切，您的过去，现在，甚至是将来，瓦西里” 低语说到。

“You may think we know everything about you—your past, present, and even future, Vasily,” the whisper continued.

瓦西里感到自己额头上的毛孔在慢慢舒张，排出那些带着紧张信息素的液体。他咽了咽口水，试探着问到“「你们」是谁？”

Vasily felt the pores on his forehead slowly expanding, excreting sweat laced with tension pheromones. He swallowed and tentatively asked, “Who are ‘you’?”

电脑的另一头沉默了片刻，又开始了浑浊的低语“在你们这个物种的诸多阴谋论中，我们的生活地点成了唯一能够描绘我们的形容词，就如同我们在电脑荧幕前信息的落款上揭示的那样——「地心人」”

The other end of the computer was silent for a moment before the murky whisper resumed, “In the many conspiracy theories of your species, the only adjective that can describe us is our dwelling place, as revealed by the sign-off on the screen—‘subterraneans.’”

瓦西里不响。

Vasily remained silent.

“但是这样的代号和你们法律的标题一样往往与事实相距甚远，不是吗？” 低语感知到了瓦西里的沉默又补充道。“其实，我们真正的身份被你们狂热又虚伪的神职传播了几千年。这个世界就像你在基辅读小学时第一次读到炼金术书上写的一样「如其在上，如其在下」。作为「下」的我们，与作为「上」的「祂们」，并没有什么不同。我们的真实身份你再熟悉不过，就是主日学校里被老师们拿来吓唬不听话的学生的那个工具……”

“But isn’t that code name, like your legal titles, often far from the truth?” The whisper, sensing Vasily’s silence, added, “Our true identity has been spread by your zealous and hypocritical clergy for thousands of years. This world is as you first read in alchemy books in primary school: ‘As above, so below.’ We, as the ‘below,’ are no different from the ‘above.’ Our true identity is familiar to you; it’s the tool Sunday school teachers use to scare disobedient students…

“你们称呼我们为「撒旦」。”

“You call us Satan.”

瓦西里的喉结像是被荆棘困住了一样无法动弹。

Vasily’s Adam’s apple felt as if it were ensnared by thorns, unable to move.
